MonoAvatar: Learning Implicit Digital Avatar from a Monocular Video

Anonymous submission

MonoAvatar takes a single monocular RGB video as input and builds a high-quality digital avatar.

Abstract

Learning digital human avatars from RGB videos is a challenging but valuable task. Most recent works try to build exquisite avatars using multi-view or 3D inputs, but their performances degrade dramatically when applied to a single monocular RGB video. In this paper, we propose a novel implicit method, named MonoAvatar, for learning digital human avatars with both high-quality geometry and life-like appearance from monocular videos. In general, we use a neural radiance field (NeRF) to represent the canonical avatar and train our model using volume rendering method. Due to the coupling of geometry and appearance in implicit representation, we demonstrate that better geometry leads to better synthesis results. Based on this theory, we propose to guide the geometry of the canonical avatar using a body-guided normal estimation network (NEN), which helps obtain high-quality reconstruction and rendering results. In addition, we propose a neural optimization field to optimize the inaccurate initial SMPL parameters and a neural detail field to model the pose-dependent deformation, both of which are important to render clearer details. As a result, our method achieves superior performance to existing methods on multiple datasets and various videos.
